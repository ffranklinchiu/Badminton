Icons of Badminton Evolution

Lee Chong Wei

Malaysian legend known for his incredible speed and consistency, dominating the sport for over a decade. Ranked world No. 1 for 349 weeks, one of the longest reigns in badminton history.

Peter Gade

Danish maestro who brought European flair to the sport during the 2000s with his tactical brilliance. Known for his deceptive strokes and innovative play style that influenced generations.

Chen Yu Fei

Chinese star representing the new generation of powerful, athletic players transforming the game. Olympic gold medalist known for her aggressive style and exceptional court coverage.

Viktor Axelsen

Current Danish superstar showcasing the modern evolution with his towering presence and technical mastery. Olympic champion who combines power with precision in the modern game.

Key Milestones in Badminton

1873

Birth of Modern Badminton

The game was first played at Badminton House in Gloucestershire, England, home of the Duke of Beaufort. The sport gets its name from this location.

1893

First Official Rules

The Badminton Association of England published the first official set of rules, standardizing the game that had previously varied by location.

1934

International Badminton Federation

The IBF (now BWF) was founded with 9 member countries: Canada, Denmark, England, France, Ireland, Netherlands, New Zealand, Scotland, and Wales.

1948-1949

First Major Tournaments

The first Thomas Cup (men's world team championship) held in 1948-49. The Uber Cup (women's equivalent) followed in 1956-57.

1972

Olympic Demonstration Sport

Badminton debuted as a demonstration sport at the Munich Olympics, paving the way for future inclusion.

1992

Olympic Recognition

Badminton became a full medal sport at the Barcelona Olympics with singles and doubles events.

1996

Mixed Doubles Added

The Atlanta Olympics introduced mixed doubles, completing badminton's Olympic program.

2006

Scoring System Revolution

The BWF introduced the 3x21 rally point scoring system, replacing the traditional 2x15 system to make games more exciting.

2020

New Serve Rule

Fixed height serve rule implemented (1.15m from court surface) to standardize serves and reduce advantage for taller players.
