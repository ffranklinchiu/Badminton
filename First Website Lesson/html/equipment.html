The Evolution of Badminton Gear

From wooden rackets to carbon fiber technology

Equipment Through the Ages

Rackets

Early rackets were made of wood with gut strings. Modern rackets use lightweight carbon fiber composites with synthetic strings, allowing for greater power and precision. The shape evolved from oval to isometric heads for larger sweet spots.

Shuttlecocks

Traditional shuttlecocks used 16 goose feathers. Modern feather shuttles still follow this design, while synthetic nylon shuttles were developed for durability. The cork base has been standardized to 25-28mm diameter with precise weight specifications.

Footwear

Players originally wore regular sports shoes. Modern badminton shoes feature gum rubber soles for grip, reinforced toes for drag steps, and cushioning for jumps. The lightweight design supports rapid multidirectional movements essential for the sport.
